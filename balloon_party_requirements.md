# Requirements Document: Small Party in a Balloon

## Overview
This document outlines the requirements and specifications for hosting a small party inside a hot air balloon. The event aims to provide a unique and memorable experience for a limited number of guests while ensuring safety, comfort, and entertainment.

## 1. Balloon Specifications

### 1.1 Size and Capacity
- **Balloon Size**: Standard hot air balloon with a minimum basket size of 1.5m x 1.5m
- **Maximum Capacity**: 6-8 people (including pilot/operator)
- **Weight Limit**: Total passenger weight not to exceed balloon manufacturer's specifications (typically 800-1000kg)

### 1.2 Safety Features
- **Emergency Landing Equipment**: Parachute system, fire extinguisher, first aid kit
- **Communication Devices**: Two-way radios, mobile phones with signal boosters
- **Weather Monitoring**: Portable weather station, access to real-time weather updates
- **Tethering Option**: Ability to tether the balloon for stationary hovering if required

## 2. Venue and Logistics

### 2.1 Launch Site Requirements
- **Clear Area**: Minimum 30m x 30m clear, flat area for inflation and launch
- **Wind Conditions**: Maximum wind speed of 7 mph (11 km/h) for safe operation
- **Obstacles**: No power lines, tall trees, or buildings within 100m of launch site
- **Landing Sites**: Pre-identified potential landing zones within flight path

### 2.2 Timing and Duration
- **Total Experience**: 3-4 hours (including pre-flight briefing and post-flight celebration)
- **Actual Flight Time**: 1-2 hours, depending on weather conditions
- **Optimal Launch Time**: Early morning (6-8 AM) or late afternoon (4-6 PM) for best weather conditions

### 2.3 Permits and Legal Requirements
- **Aviation Permits**: FAA approval or equivalent local aviation authority clearance
- **Insurance**: Specialized event and aviation insurance coverage
- **Waivers**: Signed liability waivers from all participants
- **Local Permissions**: Any required local government or property owner permissions

## 3. Guest Experience

### 3.1 Catering and Refreshments
- **Food Type**: Lightweight, easy-to-handle finger foods and canapés
- **Beverage Service**: Champagne and non-alcoholic options in shatterproof glassware
- **Storage**: Compact, secure storage for food and beverages
- **Waste Management**: Sealed containers for all waste, "leave no trace" policy

### 3.2 Entertainment
- **Music**: Portable, battery-powered speaker system with curated playlist
- **Photography**: Dedicated photographer or mounted cameras for hands-free operation
- **Special Moments**: Planned moments for toasts, announcements, or celebrations
- **Viewing Equipment**: Binoculars for scenic viewing

### 3.3 Comfort Considerations
- **Seating**: Foldable, lightweight seating options secured to basket
- **Temperature Control**: Portable heaters for cold weather or fans for hot weather
- **Accessibility**: Step stools and assistance for entering/exiting the basket
- **Motion Sickness**: Anti-nausea medication available for guests

## 4. Staffing Requirements

### 4.1 Essential Personnel
- **Pilot**: FAA-certified hot air balloon pilot with minimum 500 hours experience
- **Ground Crew**: Minimum 2 crew members for setup, takedown, and chase vehicle operation
- **Event Coordinator**: Dedicated person to manage guest experience and logistics
- **Caterer/Server**: Person responsible for food and beverage service

### 4.2 Training and Briefing
- **Safety Briefing**: Comprehensive safety instructions for all guests before launch
- **Emergency Procedures**: Clear protocols for various emergency scenarios
- **Staff Coordination**: Pre-event briefing for all staff members
- **Communication Plan**: Established communication methods between balloon and ground

## 5. Technical Requirements

### 5.1 Power and Lighting
- **Power Source**: Portable battery packs for all electronic devices
- **Lighting**: LED lighting for ambiance and safety, especially for evening events
- **Backup Systems**: Redundant power sources for critical equipment
- **Heat Source**: Properly maintained and inspected balloon burner system

### 5.2 Decoration and Theming
- **Weight Considerations**: All decorations must be lightweight and securely fastened
- **Fire Safety**: No flammable decorations near the burner
- **Exterior Visibility**: Any external decorations must not interfere with pilot visibility
- **Theme Integration**: Cohesive theme elements that work within the constraints of the balloon

## 6. Contingency Planning

### 6.1 Weather Contingencies
- **Rescheduling Policy**: Clear terms for weather-related postponement
- **Alternative Venue**: Backup ground-based venue in case of flight cancellation
- **Weather Monitoring**: Continuous monitoring before and during the event
- **Early Termination Protocol**: Procedure for safely ending the flight early if conditions change

### 6.2 Emergency Procedures
- **Medical Emergencies**: Protocol for handling medical issues during flight
- **Technical Failures**: Procedures for various balloon equipment malfunctions
- **Communication Failures**: Backup communication methods
- **Evacuation Plan**: Clear procedure for emergency landings and evacuation

## 7. Budget Considerations

### 7.1 Essential Costs
- **Balloon Rental**: Including pilot, equipment, and fuel
- **Permits and Insurance**: All required legal documentation
- **Catering**: Food, beverages, and service
- **Staffing**: All required personnel

### 7.2 Optional Enhancements
- **Photography/Videography**: Professional documentation of the event
- **Custom Branding**: Personalized balloon banners or basket decorations
- **Luxury Upgrades**: Premium catering, champagne service, gift bags
- **Extended Experience**: Additional ground-based activities before or after flight

## 8. Post-Event Considerations

### 8.1 Debriefing and Feedback
- **Guest Feedback**: Method for collecting guest impressions and suggestions
- **Staff Debrief**: Post-event review with all team members
- **Documentation**: Compilation of photos, videos, and event highlights
- **Follow-up**: Thank you notes or digital mementos for guests

### 8.2 Environmental Impact
- **Waste Management**: Proper disposal of all waste generated
- **Carbon Offset**: Optional carbon offset purchase for environmental impact
- **Site Restoration**: Ensuring launch and landing sites are left in original condition
- **Sustainable Practices**: Use of eco-friendly products where possible

## Conclusion
This requirements document provides a comprehensive framework for planning and executing a small party in a hot air balloon. Due to the unique nature of the venue, special attention must be paid to safety, weight restrictions, and weather contingencies. With proper planning and execution, this event will provide an extraordinary and memorable experience for all guests.
